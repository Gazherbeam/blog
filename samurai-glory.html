Minamoto no Yoshitsune

  • Era: Late Heian
  • Clan: Minamoto
  • Notable Feats: Brilliant general of the Genpei War, led the Minamoto to victory at the Battle of Dan-no-ura, legendary tactician and tragic hero of Japanese history.

Minamoto no Yoshitsune (1159–1189) is celebrated as one of Japan’s greatest and most tragic samurai. His exploits and loyalty have inspired countless legends, plays, and artworks.

Oda Nobunaga

  • Era: Sengoku
  • Clan: Oda
  • Notable Feats: Initiated the unification of Japan, innovative military leader, known for his ruthless tactics and modernization.

Oda Nobunaga (1534–1582) was a powerful daimyo who set Japan on the path to unification. His bold strategies and reforms changed the course of Japanese history.

Tokugawa Ieyasu

  • Era: Azuchi–Momoyama / Edo
  • Clan: Tokugawa
  • Notable Feats: Founder and first shogun of the Tokugawa shogunate, ruled Japan for over 250 years of peace and stability.

Tokugawa Ieyasu (1543–1616) unified Japan after the Battle of Sekigahara and established a dynasty that would rule for centuries.

Date Masamune

  • Era: Azuchi–Momoyama / Early Edo
  • Clan: Date
  • Notable Feats: Known as the "One-Eyed Dragon," famed for his leadership, ambition, and distinctive crescent-moon helmet.

Date Masamune (1567–1636) was a legendary daimyo of northern Japan, remembered for his military prowess and cultural influence.
